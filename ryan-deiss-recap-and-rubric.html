2. The Confidence Rubric

A 1–5 gut score recorded against every idea. Not a gate at first — we ship per the N/B strategy. After three months we pull the data and check whether high-confidence ideas actually outperformed. The whole point is to calibrate intuition over time.

ScoreMeaningWhat it feels like
5 Almost certain banger The kind of idea you'd bet money on. Genuinely surprised if it underperformed.
4 Strong belief Solid hunch this performs above channel average. Disappointed, not shocked, if it flops.
3 Could go either way No strong feeling. Even odds.
2 Doubtful but worth shooting Gut says probably no, but business reason to make it (ICP precision, sales asset). Trading reach for something else.
1 Don't make it Gut says no, no business reason to override. Killed at idea stage — but we still record the score so the retro can confirm.

How we use it

  • It's a ranking system, not a gate. For now, we ship per the N/B strategy regardless of score. The score gets recorded, not gated on.
  • Two scorers, independent. You and I each score every idea blind to each other, then reconcile at the pre-publish checkpoint. Over time the retro tells us whose gut tracks reality more closely on this channel.
  • Pure gut. No criteria checklist. Designed to be backwards-looking — at the 3-month retro we check whether high-confidence ideas actually outperformed, and that's where any real shipping rules get earned.
